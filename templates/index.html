Overview of Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. There are three main types of diabetes: type 1, type 2, and gestational diabetes.

Symptoms of Diabetes

  • Increased thirst
  • Frequent urination
  • Extreme hunger
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores
  • Frequent infections

Causes of Diabetes

Diabetes occurs when your body doesn't produce enough insulin or can't effectively use the insulin it does produce. The exact cause of diabetes varies by type:

  • Type 1 diabetes: Result of immune system attacking insulin-producing cells in the pancreas.
  • Type 2 diabetes: Combination of genetic and lifestyle factors.
  • Gestational diabetes: Hormonal changes during pregnancy.

Next Steps if Diagnosed

If diagnosed with diabetes, it's important to work closely with your healthcare team to manage blood sugar levels. This may involve:

  • Monitoring blood sugar levels regularly.
  • Following a healthy diet and exercise plan.
  • Taking medications or insulin as prescribed.
  • Managing stress and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
  • Regular check-ups and screenings.

How to Use the Model

The Diabetes Prediction Model helps predict the likelihood of an individual developing diabetes based on various health parameters. Follow these steps to use the model:

  • Register or Log in to have acccess to the model
  • Ensure you have collected the necessary health data of the patient, including:
  • Number of pregnancies (for women)
  • Glucose levels
  • Blood pressure readings
  • Skin thickness measurements
  • Insulin levels
  • Body Mass Index (BMI)
  • Diabetes Pedigree Function (genetic influence)
  • Age
  • Input these values into the respective fields in the Prediction form.
  • Click the "Predict" button to generate a prediction based on the entered data.
  • Review the prediction result to understand the risk level of diabetes.
